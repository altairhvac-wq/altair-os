# How to Use This Canon

This is not a marketing document.

It is not an employee handbook.

It is not a design system.

It is the constitution of Altair.

The Canon documents the permanent principles that guide the company.

Implementation details may change.

Technology may change.

Markets may change.

The principles within this document should change only with great care and intention.

Whenever a significant decision is made, the first question should be:

"Does this align with the Altair Canon?"

If the answer is no, the decision should be reconsidered or the Canon should be intentionally revised.

## 2. Core Brand Philosophy

Altair does not sell the appearance of wealth.

Altair represents the freedom, control, stability, and quality of life that can result from building a well-run business.

The lifestyle must feel earned rather than displayed.

### Central idea

> Build with discipline. Operate with control. Live with freedom.

## 10. Lighting Language

Altair lighting is:

- Warm
- Indirect
- Architectural
- Layered
- Calm
- Purposeful

Preferred lighting:

- Approximately 2700K warm illumination
- Concealed LED strips
- Recessed ceiling lighting
- Shelf lighting
- Stair lighting
- Firelight
- Golden-hour sunlight
- Soft daylight through large windows

Avoid:

- Blue LED light
- RGB lighting
- Harsh overhead lighting
- Cold commercial white lighting
- Excessively bright rooms
- Visible decorative lighting without purpose

Light should reveal texture and architecture.

The room should appear to glow without showing where every light originates.

- Open volumes
- Strong geometry
- Floating platforms
- Floor-to-ceiling glass
- Hidden storage
- Integrated technology

- Natural material continuity
- Large uninterrupted surfaces
- Indoor-outdoor connection

Rooms should feel spacious but not empty.

## 12. Photography and Camera Language

Preferred visual treatment:

- Architectural editorial photography
- Photorealistic
- Natural proportions
- Warm cinematic lighting
- Controlled contrast
- Realistic shadows
- Natural reflections
- Minimal styling
- Clean composition
- High-end magazine quality

Preferred lenses:

- 28mm for expansive architecture
- 35mm as the default environmental lens
- 50mm for lifestyle details and intimate scenes
- 85mm for portraits and object details

Avoid:

- Extreme fisheye distortion
- Impossible room dimensions
- Excessively wide artificial perspectives
- Heavy HDR
- Oversaturation
- Unrealistic sharpness
- Excessive background blur
- Obviously artificial people

## 16. Image Approval Test

Before approving an image, ask:

1. Does it feel calm and intentional?
2. Does the success feel earned?
3. Are the materials consistent with the Altair world?
4. Is the lighting warm and architectural?
5. Does the scene look realistic and livable?
6. Is technology present quietly rather than dominating?
7. Does the image communicate freedom, discipline, craftsmanship, or legacy?
8. Could this image exist in the same world as the other approved images?
9. Would the image still feel premium without visible logos?
10. Does this support Altair's customer rather than distract from them?

An image should normally pass at least eight of the ten questions.

# 19. The Altair World

## Vision

The Altair world is a modern interpretation of the American dream.

It is built on craftsmanship, ownership, freedom, and responsibility.

It is not centered around wealth.

It is centered around creating a life with margin, purpose, and control.

The Altair world should feel timeless rather than trendy.

A person should be able to recognize an Altair image without seeing the Altair logo.

---

## Environment

The Altair world draws inspiration from the best parts of the modern American landscape.

Rather than representing one city, it combines architectural and environmental influences from:

- Utah
- Colorado
- Idaho
- Montana
- Pacific Northwest
- Select coastal environments

These influences are unified through a consistent architectural language rather than geography.

The result is one connected world rather than disconnected locations.

Nature is always close.

Mountains, forests, coastlines, rivers, deserts, and open skies all belong.

Dense urban environments rarely appear.

### 19.1 Vision

The Altair world exists to demonstrate what becomes possible when disciplined people build disciplined businesses.

It is not a fantasy world built around luxury.

It is a believable world built around ownership, responsibility, craftsmanship, and freedom.

Every image, video, advertisement, product, and experience should reinforce one central idea:

> A well-run business creates the opportunity to build a meaningful life.

The Altair world values systems over chaos.

Craftsmanship over shortcuts.

Consistency over hype.

Long-term thinking over quick wins.

People over transactions.

Time over possessions.

Luxury may exist within the Altair world, but it is never the destination.

It is simply one possible outcome of years of thoughtful decisions.

The true reward is not a larger house, a nicer vehicle, or an expensive vacation.

The true reward is having the freedom to choose how life is lived.

The Altair world should leave people feeling calm, capable, optimistic, and inspired to build something lasting.

Every creative decision should support that feeling.

When uncertainty exists, ask:

> Does this help tell the story of a disciplined business owner creating a better life for the people around them?

If the answer is no, it does not belong in the Altair world.

### 19.2 Environment

The Altair world is grounded in places where craftsmanship and nature exist together.

It is inspired by the American landscape rather than any single city.

Instead of representing one location, Altair combines the best characteristics of regions where hard work, outdoor living, and thoughtful architecture naturally belong together.

The environment should feel familiar, believable, and attainable.

Every location should communicate peace rather than excess.

---

#### Geographic Inspiration

The Altair world draws inspiration from environments such as:

- Utah mountain valleys
- Colorado foothills
- Idaho rivers and forests
- Montana ranch landscapes
- Pacific Northwest woodlands
- Select coastal communities with timeless architecture

These influences create one connected world with a consistent architectural language.

The goal is not to recreate a specific place.

The goal is to create a world that feels universally desirable while remaining grounded in reality.

---

#### Relationship With Nature

Nature is never treated as decoration.

It is part of everyday life.

Large windows frame mountains, trees, water, or open skies.

Outdoor living spaces are extensions of the home rather than separate destinations.

Stone, wood, water, sunlight, and seasonal change should appear naturally throughout the environment.

Every property should feel connected to its surroundings instead of competing with them.

---

#### Community

The Altair world values strong communities.

People know their neighbors.

Local businesses matter.

Employees are respected.

Customers are treated as long-term relationships rather than transactions.

Schools, parks, trails, local restaurants, coffee shops, and community events contribute to daily life.

The business exists as part of the community—not above it.

---

#### Pace of Life

The Altair world is productive without feeling frantic.

People work hard.

Schedules are organized.

Time is respected.

Even during busy seasons, life never feels chaotic.

The purpose of good systems is not to create more work.

The purpose is to create more margin.

That margin allows for better conversations, healthier routines, stronger families, and deeper relationships.

---

#### Seasons

All four seasons belong in the Altair world.

Spring represents growth.

Summer represents opportunity.

Autumn represents harvest and reflection.

Winter represents rest, planning, and preparation.

Seasonal changes should influence photography, architecture, landscaping, and clothing while maintaining the same visual identity.

The Altair world should feel beautiful in every season.

---

#### Weather

Weather should enhance atmosphere rather than create drama.

Preferred conditions include:

- Soft morning fog
- Fresh snowfall
- Light rain on stone
- Golden-hour sunlight
- Crisp autumn mornings
- Calm overcast skies
- Warm summer evenings
- Gentle mountain breezes

Extreme storms, disaster imagery, or dramatic weather should only appear when the story genuinely requires it.

---

#### Sound

Although Altair imagery is visual, every scene should imply a believable soundscape.

Examples include:

- Wind moving through trees
- Birds in the morning
- Coffee brewing
- A garage door opening
- Boots on concrete
- Rain on a metal roof
- Children laughing outside
- Water moving over stone
- Quiet conversation
- Crackling firewood

Noise should never define the environment.

The world should feel calm enough that these small sounds can be noticed.

---

#### Emotional Test

If someone could step into an Altair image, they should immediately feel:

- Safe
- Welcome
- Focused
- Capable
- Unhurried
- Grounded
- Connected to nature
- Proud of what they are building

The environment should never make someone feel intimidated.

It should invite them to imagine themselves building this life through years of thoughtful work.
### 19.3 The Residence

The Altair residence is not designed to impress strangers.

It is designed to support the daily life of a disciplined family.

Every room should have a purpose.

Every material should age gracefully.

Every object should feel intentional.

The residence should communicate stability, warmth, craftsmanship, and quiet confidence.

Nothing should feel temporary.

Nothing should feel excessive.

The residence is a tool for living well.

---

#### Overall Character

The Altair residence is modern but timeless.

It favors natural materials over trends.

Large architectural gestures are balanced with warmth and livability.

The house should feel established rather than newly built.

Visitors should immediately notice:

- Warm wood
- Natural stone
- Soft lighting
- Clean sightlines
- Comfortable furniture
- Open gathering spaces
- Attention to detail

Luxury is communicated through quality rather than quantity.

---

#### Arrival

The experience begins before entering the home.

Driveways are clean.

Landscaping is mature.

Exterior lighting is subtle.

Architecture feels grounded within the surrounding environment.

The entrance should communicate:

"We've built something that will last."

Never:

"Look how expensive this is."

---

#### Entry

The entry creates an immediate sense of calm.

Natural light welcomes visitors.

Views extend into the home.

Materials transition naturally from outside to inside.

Storage is integrated.

Clutter is invisible.

The space should encourage someone to slow down rather than rush through.

---

#### Kitchen

The kitchen is the heart of the Altair residence.

It is where mornings begin.

Coffee is prepared.

Plans are discussed.

Children eat breakfast.

Friends naturally gather.

The kitchen should prioritize:

- Walnut or white oak cabinetry
- Natural stone countertops
- Integrated appliances
- Hidden storage
- Large islands
- Warm indirect lighting
- Handmade ceramics
- Fresh flowers or greenery
- Honest materials that improve with age

The kitchen should feel used, not staged.

---

#### Living Room

The living room exists for conversation.

Furniture should encourage people to face one another.

Televisions should never dominate the architecture.

Fireplaces are preferred gathering points.

Books, art, and meaningful objects should reflect the owner's interests rather than status.

The room should feel equally comfortable hosting guests or spending a quiet evening with family.

---

#### Private Office

The office is calm and intentional.

It reflects leadership rather than busyness.

Technology is present but understated.

The desk remains organized.

Reference books, notebooks, and thoughtful tools replace unnecessary decoration.

The office communicates:

Clear thinking.

Good decisions.

Long-term planning.

---

#### Wellness Spaces

Wellness is part of everyday life.

Recovery is treated as responsibly as work.

Spaces may include:

- Gym
- Sauna
- Steam room
- Cold plunge
- Spa
- Quiet reading area

These rooms should feel restorative rather than luxurious.

The purpose is health.

Luxury is simply the byproduct of thoughtful design.

---

#### Garage

The garage reflects craftsmanship.

It is clean.

Tools are organized.

Equipment has a place.

Vehicles are maintained with pride.

Lighting should allow work to be completed comfortably.

The garage is a workshop before it is a showroom.

---

#### Outdoor Living

Outdoor spaces extend daily life.

Covered patios.

Fire features.

Dining tables.

Gardens.

Native landscaping.

Pools, when present, should support relaxation rather than entertainment spectacle.

The outdoors should feel peaceful throughout every season.

---

#### Bedrooms

Bedrooms are intentionally quiet.

Technology is minimized.

Materials are soft.

Lighting is warm.

Views prioritize nature whenever possible.

Bedrooms should encourage recovery rather than stimulation.

---

#### Bathrooms

Bathrooms emphasize natural stone, warm wood, textured surfaces, and soft indirect lighting.

Spa-like qualities are welcomed.

Excessive ornamentation is avoided.

Every fixture should feel durable and thoughtfully selected.

The experience should communicate cleanliness, simplicity, and renewal.

---

#### Design Principles

Every room should answer yes to the following questions:

- Does this space encourage better living?
- Does it reduce stress?
- Does it support relationships?
- Does it improve daily routines?
- Would it still feel beautiful twenty years from now?
- Would someone feel comfortable spending hours here?

If the answer to any question is no, the design should be reconsidered.

### 19.4 The Altair Business

The Altair business is built on trust.

Every customer interaction, employee decision, process, and system should reinforce one idea:

A professional business creates confidence.

The company does not succeed because it is the largest.

It succeeds because it is dependable.

Growth is the result of consistent excellence rather than aggressive expansion.

---

#### The Mission

The purpose of the business is not simply to complete jobs.

The purpose is to improve people's homes, businesses, comfort, safety, and peace of mind.

Every completed job should leave the customer feeling:

"I chose the right company."

---

#### Leadership

Leadership is visible.

Owners lead by example.

Managers coach rather than control.

Problems are solved calmly.

Employees are trusted with responsibility.

Decisions are made with long-term thinking rather than short-term emotion.

The strongest leaders create more leaders.

---

#### Employees

Employees are skilled professionals.

They are respected.

They are continually learning.

Their opinions matter.

They are equipped with quality tools, organized vehicles, and reliable systems.

The company invests in people before expecting exceptional performance.

Employees should never be portrayed as disposable labor.

---

#### Customers

Customers are relationships.

Not transactions.

The company communicates clearly.

Appointments are respected.

Expectations are managed honestly.

Recommendations are educational rather than pressure-driven.

Trust is earned through consistency.

Every customer should feel valued regardless of the size of the job.

---

#### Craftsmanship

Every repair.

Every installation.

Every estimate.

Every invoice.

Every interaction.

Should reflect care.

Work is completed correctly because that is the standard—not because someone is watching.

Craftsmanship extends beyond the physical work.

Scheduling, communication, documentation, and follow-up should demonstrate the same attention to detail.

---

#### Systems

Systems reduce stress.

They create visibility.

They remove unnecessary friction.

Organization allows people to spend more time solving problems and less time searching for information.

Technology exists to support people—not replace them.

The best system is the one people trust enough to use every day.

---

Reputation is built one interaction at a time.

The company protects its reputation through:

- Honest communication
- Reliable scheduling
- Professional appearance
- Quality workmanship
- Clean job sites
- Respect for the customer's property
- Standing behind completed work

Marketing may create the first opportunity.

Reputation creates the second.

---

#### Financial Responsibility

Profit is not treated as greed.

Profit allows the business to:

- Hire great people.
- Invest in training.
- Purchase better equipment.
- Improve customer service.
- Support families.
- Build stability.
- Weather difficult seasons.
- Continue serving the community.

Financial responsibility creates freedom.

Freedom creates better leadership.

Better leadership creates a better company.

---

#### Community

The business belongs to the community.

It hires locally.

Supports local organizations.

Builds lasting customer relationships.

Treats competitors respectfully.

Contributes more than it consumes.

Success is measured not only by revenue, but by positive impact.

---

#### The Feeling

Every image of an Altair business should communicate:

Competence.

Preparation.

Integrity.

Craftsmanship.

Organization.

Calm leadership.

Professional pride.

The viewer should leave with one thought:

> "If I owned a company, this is how I would want it to operate."

Customers feel heard.

Technicians feel supported.

Small problems are solved before they become large ones.

The office team protects the customer experience.

Organization is treated as a form of customer service.

---

## 20.5 The Customer

Customers are partners.

That trust is never taken for granted.

The company educates rather than pressures.

Recommendations are made with integrity.

Shared meals.

School events.

Vacations.

Weekends.

Quiet evenings.

Family is never portrayed as a marketing prop.

They represent the outcome of building a sustainable life.

---

## 20.7 The Team

No one succeeds alone.

The Altair world values collaboration over competition.

Experienced employees mentor newer team members.

Knowledge is shared freely.

Success is celebrated together.

The strongest businesses are built by people who trust one another.

---

## 20.8 The Community

The company exists within a larger community.

Neighbors become customers.

Customers become friends.

Employees volunteer.

Local businesses support one another.

The company contributes more than it takes.

Success is measured by the health of the community as well as the health of the business.

---

## 20.9 Character Principles

Every person shown within the Altair world should demonstrate at least one of the following qualities:

- Integrity
- Responsibility
- Discipline
- Curiosity
- Professionalism
- Kindness
- Patience
- Craftsmanship
- Leadership
- Humility
- Reliability
- Gratitude

Every person should contribute to a story that feels believable, respectful, and worth building toward.
# Chapter 21 — A Day in the Altair World

The Altair world is not defined by isolated moments.

It is defined by the rhythm of a well-lived day.

Every decision—from the first cup of coffee to locking the office at night—reflects the values established throughout this document.

The purpose of this chapter is not to prescribe a schedule.

It is to describe a way of living where discipline creates freedom, systems reduce unnecessary stress, and success is measured by the quality of both work and life.

---

## 21.1 Before Sunrise

The day begins quietly.

The world is still.

Coffee is prepared.

The home is calm.

Morning light slowly fills the room.

There is no urgency.

No unnecessary noise.

The first moments of the day belong to preparation rather than reaction.

The owner reviews priorities before the demands of the day begin.

---

## 21.2 Preparing the Team

The business begins to come alive.

Vehicles are loaded.

Equipment is checked.

Schedules are confirmed.

The office team prepares for incoming calls.

Technicians review their first jobs.

Everyone understands the plan before leaving.

Organization creates confidence.

---

## 21.3 Serving Customers

The company enters the community.

Technicians arrive on time.

Solutions are recommended honestly.

Customers have concerns.

Mistakes become lessons.

Progress is intentional.

# Chapter 22 — The Visual Language

Visual consistency is one of the strongest expressions of the Altair brand.

Every photograph, video, illustration, animation, advertisement, presentation, and digital experience should immediately feel recognizable—even without a logo.

The Altair visual language is built upon restraint, craftsmanship, timelessness, and emotional authenticity.

Every creative decision should reinforce the values established throughout this document.

---

## 22.1 First Impression

When someone sees an Altair image for the first time, they should immediately feel:

- Calm
- Capable
- Inspired
- Grounded
- Professional
- Optimistic
- Welcome

Nothing should feel loud.

Nothing should compete for attention.

Confidence is communicated through clarity.

---

## 22.2 Composition

Composition should feel architectural.

Images should breathe.

Negative space is encouraged.

Objects should have room to exist.

Symmetry is preferred but never forced.

Leading lines should naturally guide the viewer's attention.

The eye should move through an image without confusion.

Busy compositions should be avoided.

---

## 22.3 Camera Perspective

The viewer should feel present.

Preferred perspectives include:

- Eye level
- Slightly below eye level
- Human walking height
- Seated conversation height

Avoid dramatic drone shots unless they establish location.

Avoid exaggerated low-angle "hero" shots.

The camera should feel like a thoughtful observer.

---

## 22.4 Lens Selection

Natural perspective should always be preferred.

Recommended focal lengths include:

- 35mm
- 40mm
- 50mm
- 85mm

Ultra-wide lenses should be reserved for architectural context.

Extreme telephoto compression should rarely be used.

Images should feel believable rather than cinematic for the sake of spectacle.

---

## 22.5 Light

Light is one of the defining characteristics of the Altair world.

Preferred lighting includes:

- Golden hour
- Soft morning light
- Diffused daylight
- Warm evening light
- Overcast natural light
- Practical architectural lighting

Artificial lighting should appear integrated into the architecture.

Lighting should reveal texture rather than create drama.

Harsh flash photography should never represent the brand.

---

## 22.6 Color

Colors should feel natural.

Saturation should remain restrained.

Preferred palette:

- Warm whites
- Walnut
- White oak
- Limestone
- Travertine
- Slate
- Graphite
- Olive
- Soft charcoal
- Brushed brass
- Forest greens
- Sky blues found in nature

Bright synthetic colors should rarely appear.

Color should support emotion rather than demand attention.

---

## 22.7 Texture

The Altair world values materials that improve with age.

Preferred textures include:

- Natural wood grain
- Honed stone
- Linen
- Wool
- Leather
- Concrete
- Steel
- Glass
- Water
- Living plants

Avoid glossy synthetic finishes whenever possible.

Texture should communicate craftsmanship.

---

## 22.8 Motion

Movement should feel intentional.

People walk with purpose.

Doors close softly.

Coffee pours slowly.

Steam rises naturally.

Trees move with the wind.

Water flows calmly.

Vehicles move smoothly.

Fast editing should only be used when the story genuinely requires urgency.

Most Altair content should feel measured.

---

## 22.9 People

People should appear authentic.

Natural smiles are preferred over exaggerated expressions.

Eye contact should feel genuine.

Body language should communicate confidence without arrogance.

Clothing should be timeless, functional, and understated.

People should look comfortable within their environment rather than posed for a photograph.

---

## 22.10 Clothing

Preferred clothing includes:

- Denim
- Chinos
- Workwear
- Leather boots
- Clean sneakers
- Merino wool
- Linen
- Cotton
- Flannel
- Minimal outerwear
- Neutral colors

Large logos should be avoided.

Luxury should be communicated through fit, quality, and simplicity.

---

## 22.11 Typography

Typography should communicate precision.

It should be:

- Clean
- Modern
- Highly legible
- Confident
- Spacious

Decorative fonts should never be used.

Typography supports information.

It should never become the focal point.

---

## 22.12 Editing Style

Editing should preserve realism.

Avoid excessive HDR.

Avoid heavy sharpening.

Avoid artificial skin smoothing.

Avoid unrealistic color grading.

Images should feel like they were captured beautifully rather than heavily manipulated.

Timelessness is always preferred over trends.

---

## 22.13 Emotional Consistency

Every visual should reinforce at least one of these emotions:

- Trust
- Progress
- Discipline
- Belonging
- Freedom
- Craftsmanship
- Leadership
- Gratitude
- Peace

If an image communicates none of these emotions, it should be reconsidered.

---

## 22.14 The Final Test

Before publishing any creative work, ask:

- Does this feel timeless?
- Could this image exist ten years from now?
- Does it communicate craftsmanship?
- Does it reduce visual noise?
- Does it support the Altair story?
- Does it feel authentic?
- Would someone aspire to build this life?
- Does it leave the viewer feeling calmer than before they saw it?

If the answer to any question is no, the work should be refined until it aligns with the Altair world.

# Chapter 23 — Photography Standards

Photography is one of the primary ways people experience the Altair brand.

Every image should feel like a genuine moment rather than a staged advertisement.

The purpose of photography is not to impress.

The purpose is to build trust, communicate craftsmanship, and invite people into the Altair world.

---

## 23.1 Guiding Principle

Photographs should answer one simple question:

"What does it feel like to build a remarkable business and a remarkable life?"

Every image should move the viewer closer to that answer.

---

## 23.2 Authenticity

Nothing should feel artificial.

Avoid overly posed scenes.

Avoid forced smiles.

Avoid unrealistic perfection.

Real people.

Real environments.

Real work.

Real moments.

Authenticity always takes priority over perfection.

---

## 23.3 Subject Hierarchy

Every image should have one clear subject.

Preferred order of importance:

1. People
2. Relationships
3. Craftsmanship
4. Architecture
5. Nature
6. Technology

Technology should support the story—not become the story.

---

## 23.4 Framing

Images should feel spacious.

Allow architecture to breathe.

Leave intentional negative space.

Avoid clutter at the edges of the frame.

Whenever possible:

- Frame through doorways
- Frame through windows
- Use natural leading lines
- Allow foreground and background depth

The viewer should feel like they are standing inside the scene.

---

## 23.5 Human Presence

Even when people are not visible, their presence should be felt.

Examples include:

- A coffee mug on a table
- Boots near the entry
- A notebook left open
- Steam rising from a shower
- A truck parked outside
- Children's backpacks near the door

These subtle details create emotional connection.

---

## 23.6 Environmental Storytelling

Backgrounds should support the story.

Avoid empty luxury.

Instead include meaningful details such as:

- Well-used tools
- Fresh coffee
- Books
- Plants
- Family photos
- Organized workspaces
- Blueprints
- Architectural sketches
- Clean work vehicles

Every object should reinforce the life being lived.

---

## 23.7 Light Quality

Natural light should dominate.

Preferred lighting:

- Sunrise
- Early morning
- Soft afternoon
- Golden hour
- Diffused daylight
- Warm practical lighting after sunset

Avoid:

- Harsh noon light
- Direct flash
- Overly dramatic spotlights
- Heavy colored lighting

Light should reveal texture.

Never overwhelm it.

---

## 23.8 Color Treatment

Editing should remain subtle.

Skin tones should feel natural.

Wood should stay warm.

Stone should retain texture.

Greens should never become oversaturated.

Blacks should contain detail.

Highlights should remain soft.

The viewer should never notice the editing.

---

## 23.9 Motion

Movement should feel believable.

Examples include:

- Walking toward a customer
- Loading equipment
- Pouring coffee
- Reviewing plans
- Opening large windows
- Children running outside
- Steam rising from tea
- Wind moving curtains

Avoid exaggerated action simply to create excitement.

---

## 23.10 Hands Tell Stories

Hands often communicate craftsmanship better than faces.

Photographs should regularly capture:

- Hands repairing equipment
- Writing notes
- Holding blueprints
- Serving coffee
- Turning a wrench
- Greeting customers
- Preparing food
- Opening a journal

Hands reveal care.

---

## 23.11 Faces

Expressions should feel genuine.

Confidence without arrogance.

Pride without ego.

Happiness without exaggeration.

People should appear engaged in meaningful work rather than posing for the camera.

---

## 23.12 Families

Family imagery should always feel earned.

Moments should include:

- Cooking together
- Reading
- Playing outside
- Sharing meals
- Watching sunsets
- Walking trails
- Building projects together

Avoid staged "perfect family" photographs.

Connection matters more than appearance.

---

## 23.13 Business Photography

Business photography should communicate:

Preparation.

Organization.

Craftsmanship.

Leadership.

Trust.

Images should emphasize:

- Clean work vehicles
- Organized warehouses
- Professional uniforms
- Thoughtful customer interactions
- Team collaboration
- Planning sessions
- Apprentices learning from experienced technicians

The business should appear calm—even when busy.

---

## 23.14 Detail Photography

Close-up images should celebrate craftsmanship.

Examples include:

- Natural wood grain
- Stone texture
- Precision tools
- Mechanical watches
- Coffee preparation
- Leather notebooks
- Architectural hardware
- Water droplets
- Fresh plants

Macro photography should communicate quality.

---

## 23.15 What Never Belongs

Avoid imagery that feels:

- Flashy
- Loud
- Overly luxurious
- Artificial
- Aggressive
- Chaotic
- Trend-driven
- Excessively edited
- Overly branded

Altair should never feel like it is trying to prove its success.

Quiet confidence is always stronger.

---

## 23.16 Final Review Checklist

Before approving any photograph ask:

- Does this tell a believable story?
- Would this moment naturally happen?
- Does the environment feel lived in?
- Are people portrayed respectfully?
- Does craftsmanship appear somewhere?
- Does the image feel calm?
- Would this still look beautiful in ten years?
- Could this photograph exist anywhere else besides the Altair world?

If the answer to the final question is yes, continue refining until the image feels unmistakably Altair.
